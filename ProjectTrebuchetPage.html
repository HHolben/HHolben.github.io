Building a Trebuchet

After I fell in love with electronic circuitry at age 12, I spent many hours gawking- nearly drooling over the books in Barnes and Nobel's science books section. I was especially drawn towards the books written for makers and DIY enthusiasts. As a treat for me, my mother would take me there sometimes after school on fridays. On one such trip, she purchased Simon Monk's book, 15 Dangerously Mad Projects For the Evil Genius for me.

The book's humorous tone made the book enjoyable for me but for a long time I didn't do a single project in the book. I just read through the details of how the projects that interested me were done. One of those dangerously mad projects was a trebuchet. I had heard about trebuchet's from a close friend of mine who built a model trebuchet for a science fair project. I wound up making a small catapult out of sticks, duct tape, and sticks I picked off the lawn. I threw a few pieces of gravel with that catapult, but it fell apart quickly. I later planned to build a larger trebuchet- again just using fallen branches from my parents' yard It was not until years later, when I was in college, that i finally got around to tackling the trebuchet project.

I got recertified in lifeguarding at the Kingsport aquatic center, and took a three or four week course that earned me a "Water Safety Instructor" certification. The week after the class ended, I felt inspired to be productive on my Saturday mornings- it had felt so good to get out and do something interesting before the day even really began. On the next Saturday morning, I got up early and went back to the pile of scrap wood that had once been my family's old tool shed. On weekends I began making a raised garden bed for my mother using the pile of scrap wood that remained of the old shed. After I finished that garden bed, I needed to find a new project to fill my saturday mornings with and I was reminded of the trebuchet project from 15 Dangerously Mad Projects For the Evil Genius.. I started salvaging wood out of the pile to build myself a trebuchet.

Trebuchet Test: October 2019
