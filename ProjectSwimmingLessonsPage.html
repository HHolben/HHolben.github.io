Swimming Lessons

This is an index of resources and links to resources I to organize for the swim lessons I teach.


This is a list of questions in progress for my swim lessons. If a swimmer can answer "yes" to all of these questions, then my instruction will probably not be the most helpful option to improve their swimming skills.

  1. Are you comfortable getting in the water?
  2. Can you blow bubbles?
  3. Can you open your eyes underwater without goggles?
  4. Do you understand the safety importance of learning how to swim?
  5. Do you know why its not a good idea to drink the water you are swimming in?
  6. Can you get on your back, and stay on your back?
  7. Can you get on your front and stay on your front?
  8. Do you know what a lifejacket is for?
  9. What are some situations in which you would need to know how to float?
  10. Can you float?
  11. Do you understand your lungs' use in floating, and your breathing's use in floating?
  12. Can you move around without touching the pool floor?
  13. Can you scull?
  14. Can you tread?
  15. Can you move around by kicking?
  16. Can you move around by stroking?
  17. Do you understand how a boat oar works?
  18. Do you know how to efficiently pose your hands?
  19. Do you know what the streamline position is?
  20. Can you perform different kicks and strokes?
    • Can you perform the freestyle (AKA front crawl) stroke?
    • Can you perform the breastroke?
    • Can you perform the backstroke?
    • Can you perform the elementary backstroke?
    • Can you perform the butterfly stroke?
    • Can you perform the sidestroke?
  21. Can you move around under water without touching?
  22. Can you torpedo?
  23. Do you know how to rescue someone who is distressed in still water or in rapids?
  24. Are you comfortable going in the deep end?
  25. Can you get to the bottom of the deep end?
  26. Can you use different kinds of surface dives to get to the bottom of the deep end?
  27. Do you understand the dangers of going deep?
  28. Do you know how to properly perform each swimming stroke, integrating breath, catch, power phase, and recovery phase for each kick and stroke?
  29. Can you dive?
  30. Are you comfortable using the diving board?
  31. Do you understand why diving can be dangerous, and how to dive safely?
  32. Can you perform multiple types of dives?
  33. Can you demonstrate the appropriate racing starts for each stroke?
  34. Can you demonstrate the correct racing turns for each stroke?
  35. Do you know how to use a snorkel?
  36. Do you understand the purpose of and know how to use exercise equipment like fins, pool buoys, and swim caps?
  37. Can you perform each stroke properly for extended lengths and times?
